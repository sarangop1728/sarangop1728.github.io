Supersingular Elliptic Curves

Supersingularity

Characteristic 2

In characteristic \(2\), an elliptic curve is supersingular if and only if it's \(j\) invariant is zero. One way of proving this is to use the fact that supersingular elliptic curves in characteristic \(p\) have \(j\)-invariant in \(\mathbf{F}_{p^2}\) and trivial \(p\)-torsion. This can be checked in MAGMA by calculating the \(2\)-torsion subscheme for each possible \(j \in \mathbf{F}_4\). 

> K := GF(4);
> for j in K do TorsionSubgroupScheme(EllipticCurveFromjInvariant(j),2);
end for;
Subgroup scheme of Elliptic Curve defined by y^2 + y = x^3 over GF(2^2)
defined by 1
Subgroup scheme of Elliptic Curve defined by y^2 + x*y = x^3 + 1 over
GF(2^2) defined by x^2
Subgroup scheme of Elliptic Curve defined by y^2 + x*y = x^3 + K.1^2 over
GF(2^2) defined by x^2
Subgroup scheme of Elliptic Curve defined by y^2 + x*y = x^3 + K.1 over
GF(2^2) defined by x^2

This allows us to conclude that over \(\bar{\mathbf{F}}_2\), all supersingular elliptic curves are isomorphic to \(y^2 + y = x^3\).
